Ernakulam district is situated in the central part of the state, spans an area of about 3,000 square kilometres, and is home to over 9% of Kerala's population. The Ernakulam district covers an area of 3,068 km2 on the Western Coastal Plains of India. It is surrounded by the Thrissur District to the north, the Idukki District to the east, Alappuzha and Kottayam districts to the south, the Coimbatore District of Tamilnadu state to the north-east and the Laccadive Sea to the west. The district is divided geographically into highland, midland, and coastal area. The altitude of the highlands is about 300 m. The Periyar River, Kerala's longest, flows through the district. The district has a moderate climate and mostly falls within the Malabar Coast moist forests ecoregion, while the highlands are part of the South Western Ghats moist deciduous forests ecoregion.
